What is a P/E ratio and why does it matter?
Stock valuation fundamentals for NGX investors
BeginnerStocksNGX
⏱ 5 min+100 pts on completion
📖 Quick lesson
The Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio tells you how much investors pay for every ₦1 of a company's earnings. Formula: Share price ÷ Earnings per share.

If DANGCEM trades at ₦543 and earns ₦50/share, P/E = 10.86x. Nigerian banking stocks typically trade at 3–6x P/E — much lower than US tech at 20–50x. Always compare within the same sector.

Bamboo, Risevest & what the CBN rules mean for you

📖 Quick lesson
Nigerians can invest in US stocks through SEC-licensed platforms like Bamboo, Risevest, and Trove. You fund in naira; investments are held in USD — giving you a hedge against naira devaluation. The CBN allows individuals to invest up to $10,000/year through approved channels. Always check your platform is SEC-registered.
