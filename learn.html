What is a P/E ratio and why does it matter?
Understanding stock valuation fundamentals
+100 pts
Beginner Stocks 5 min
📖 Lesson
The Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio tells you how much investors are willing to pay for every ₦1 of a company's earnings. If a stock trades at ₦100 and earns ₦10 per share, its P/E is 10x.

A lower P/E often means the stock is cheaper relative to its earnings — common in value investing. Nigerian banking stocks like GTCO typically trade at 3–6x P/E, while high-growth US tech stocks can trade at 30–60x. Context matters — always compare P/E within the same sector.

Investing in US stocks from Nigeria — full guide
Bamboo, Risevest & what the CBN rules mean for you
+150 pts
Intermediate US Stocks 8 min
📖 Lesson
Nigerians can invest in US stocks through regulated apps like Bamboo, Risevest, and Trove. You can fund in naira and your investments are denominated in USD — giving you a hedge against naira devaluation.

The CBN allows individuals to invest up to $10,000/year through approved channels. Gains and dividends are returned in USD to your wallet, and you can withdraw in naira at current rates.
